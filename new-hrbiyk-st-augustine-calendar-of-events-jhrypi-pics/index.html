Spring in St. Augustine signals a blossoming of activity. The city awakens from its winter slumber with a range of events celebrating the season’s arrival. The St. Augustine Pirate & Treasure Museum often hosts special spring events, focusing on pirate lore and artifacts. Check their website for specific dates and programs. Easter Celebrations (March/April):

Easter in St. Augustine is a time of religious observance and community celebration. Many churches host special Easter services, and the city often features Easter egg hunts and other family-friendly activities. The atmosphere is festive, with a blend of traditional religious observances and secular celebrations. Keep an eye on local church announcements and community calendars for specific events.

Nights of Lights (November â€“ January):

Arguably St. Augustine’s most famous event, Nights of Lights transforms the city into a dazzling spectacle of light. Millions of tiny white lights adorn buildings, trees, and streets, creating a magical ambiance that captivates visitors. The event typically runs from mid-November to the end of January, providing a stunning backdrop for evening strolls through the historic district. During Nights of Lights, many businesses extend their hours, and special events, such as horse-drawn carriage rides and holiday markets, add to the festive atmosphere. This is undoubtedly a peak tourist season, so booking accommodations and making reservations well in advance is highly recommended.

St. Augustine Film Festival (Various Dates):

For film enthusiasts, the St. Augustine Film Festival is a must-see event. Showcasing independent films and documentaries, the festival provides a platform for emerging and established filmmakers. The festival typically takes place in the spring or fall, featuring screenings, workshops, and networking opportunities. Check their official website for the most up-to-date information on dates and programming.

Their gallery showcases the work of local artists, and they often organize special events and exhibitions featuring specific themes or artists. Historic Reenactments (Year-Round):

The reenactments vary in scale and frequency, but they provide an immersive and educational experience.
