The Structure of the Port St. Lucie School Calendar:

The Port St. Lucie school calendar, overseen by the St. Lucie County School District, typically follows a traditional academic year structure. This generally consists of:

  • First Day of School: The exact date varies from year to year and is usually announced several months in advance by the school district. Parents should check the official district website or their child’s school website for the most up-to-date information. The start date often falls in late August or early September.

  • Fall Break: A short break, usually lasting a few days, typically occurs in October. This provides a mid-semester respite for students and teachers.

  • Thanksgiving Break: A longer break, usually encompassing the Thanksgiving holiday, typically falls in late November. This allows families time to celebrate the holiday and enjoy a longer period of rest.

  • Winter Break/Christmas Break: This is the longest break of the year, typically spanning two to three weeks and encompassing the Christmas and New Year’s holidays. It usually begins in late December and extends into early January.

  • Spring Break: A week-long break in the spring, typically occurring in March or April, provides a much-needed recharge before the final stretch of the academic year.

  • Last Day of School: The last day of school is typically in late May or early June, marking the end of the regular academic year.

Accessing the Official Calendar:

The most reliable source for the Port St. Lucie school calendar is the official website of the St. Lucie County School District. The district’s website usually features a dedicated section for calendars, often with downloadable PDF versions for easy printing and access. Individual school websites may also post their own calendars, which might include school-specific events and activities not reflected in the district-wide calendar. It’s always advisable to check both the district and individual school websites for a complete picture of the academic schedule.
